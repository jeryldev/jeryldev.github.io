About Me

Hi there! I'm Jeryl, a software engineer who loves building useful things with code. My professional journey spans a diverse range of roles across different industries - from private fund accounting and alternative investments, to technical support for portfolio management systems and cloud-based accounting software, and now to software engineering. I've helped build payment systems for a cryptocurrency exchange and currently contribute to legal tech applications. This breadth of experience gives me a unique perspective when approaching software problems.

My Journey

I started out in a completely different world - finance! After graduating with an Accountancy degree in 2012, I was fortunate to work with financial systems that sparked my curiosity about how technology actually works. This led me down a path through technical support roles where I got to learn so much about software from the inside out. Each role taught me something valuable that I now apply to software engineering.

Why Software Engineering?

What drew me to software engineering? It's the amazing blend of creativity and problem-solving that gets me excited every day! There's something truly magical about turning an idea into working code that helps solve real problems. I'm constantly amazed by how much there is to learn in this field - every project brings new challenges and opportunities to grow. The most rewarding part is seeing something I've helped create actually making someone's day a little easier.

I'm grateful to be able to contribute to full-stack applications using tools like Elixir/Phoenix and JavaScript, work on neat features using AI technologies, and build solutions that help people in their daily work. It's a privilege to be part of creating technology that makes a difference!

I'm also taking classes toward a Graduate Diploma in Computer Science, which is helping me understand the "why" behind the "how" of programming - it's fascinating to connect theory with practical application!

I'd love to connect and learn from others in the field! Feel free to check out my LinkedIn profile if you'd like to reach out.

I completed the legacy Full Stack Certification on freeCodeCamp on May 17, 2020, and landed my first developer job in December 2020!
